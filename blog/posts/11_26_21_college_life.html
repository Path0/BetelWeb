college life

so i'm in college now.

and it's the best time of my life.


It's been about 3 and a half months since I moved into Purdue University. A lot has happened since I moved in, and I'll try to remember as much as I can so strap in this is gonna be a long post. I think. I'm actually probably too lazy to write everything out so I'll just hit the highlights.


I live in Owen Hall, and despite the bad rap, I don't actually think it's that bad. I can live with no A/C and the room itself isn't that small. I'm able to fit everything I need in it, my computer, piano keyboard, the many clocks, and more! Owen is really close to all of my classes, of which three are in Armstrong hall of Engineering, about a 5 minute walk from home. Ford Dining court is also really close, right across the street from Owen, but the food there is mediocre at best, so I don't often go there.


I've made amazing friends since I've been here. My friend Isabel (re - it's complicated) introduced me to Lauren and Amaya, who live off campus but not too far away, and from there a group was formed. They had met some people from their BGR (Boiler Gold Rush) group, basically the week-long orientation thing, who joined the group soon after. As time went on, we got aquainted with more people and soon we had a pretty good thing going on, an actual friend group that we all loved! We called ourselves


Social Friction

insert logo here once i eventually make one


Social Friction, a term coined by Aidan, defined as "the inability to complete a task due to continuous talking with one or more people or the inability to arrive at a destination due to socially interacting with one or more people one runs into on the way". Details can be found here. We get together pretty often throught each week, whether to study together at Chaney, get dinner somewhere, or to party at Lauren and Amaya's apartment. No matter what, we have a good time when we're around each other and I couldn't ask for better friends to start my college experience with.


My classes aren't too difficult as far as content goes, it's just there is a lot of work and it's literally impossible to stay caught up. As I am writing this, I have about 8 hours of lecture to watch and take notes over from the past few weeks. My engineering class, ENGR 131 is mostly business and project management, which I absolutely hate and it makes me want to die, so I have to find fulfillment through my speech teacher, Dr. Beasley. I don't really like speech as a class but she makes the class so much fun because of her style and personality, one times we skipped learning about speech to do what the previous class in that room did, learn about bones (She's an anthropologist by trade). Calculus 2 is terrible, CHEM 115 is terrible, but I just deal with it. MSE 190 I haven't even shown up to class in the last month, it's a throwaway class anyways.


To keep myself sane, I've also been making stupid things. I'll make posts about some of the things I've made in /p when I have more time. We love 3AM motivation to work on the website don't we. Not even coffee-fueled rage that's making me do this. Anyways, I've made a 6 foot long longboard I call "The Longerboard" that has gotten some attention around campus through the Purdue Longboarding Club and just random people taking pictures of it. It does look pretty stupid in comparison to actual skateboards. I just took the penny board I've had in my attic for 10 years, took the trucks off, and stuck it on a plank of wood I bought from menards. It actually works pretty well. I also made The Bomb. It's not an actual bomb, probably. At least for legal reasons it's not. I have an old lead-acid battery from the high school that I needed something to do with, so I thought I would make a portable charger with it. I wanted to give it a steampunk look, so I bought a bunch of wood and brass and have since been building it. As of right now, I'm not actually done, so I'll post about that after. Those are the major things, but I've made a goal to do something stupid like that every week I'm here. It's actually been pretty reasonable to stick to that, only missing one or two weeks since I got here.


I think that's all I really have to say tonight, but I really have been loving my time at Purdue and I am so excited to see where this goes. I've had a ton of fun, learned a lot, and developed relationships I hope never go away. If anyone in Social Friction is reading this, know you are appreciated and I love each and every one of you for being my friend. Alright, gushy stuff over, I love college and I'll probably post about anything interesting that happens over the course of the four years I'll be here.
